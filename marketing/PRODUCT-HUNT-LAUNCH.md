# ClipSync Product Hunt Launch Guide

## Preparation Phase (2-3 weeks before launch)

### Week 1: Setup & Assets

**Create Product Hunt Profile**
- Complete company profile with logo, website, social links
- Add detailed product description
- Upload product screenshots (minimum 5, maximum 10)
- Create a compelling product video (60-90 seconds)
- Set up hunter profile or prepare your own

**Required Assets Checklist:**
- [ ] Product logo (PNG, 200x200px)
- [ ] Product screenshots (minimum 5)
  1. Main dashboard view
  2. Real-time sync in action
  3. Developer transformations
  4. Team collaboration interface
  5. Mobile app interface
- [ ] Product video (60-90 seconds)
  - Hook: Problem statement (0-15s)
  - Solution: ClipSync demo (15-45s)
  - Value: Key benefits (45-60s)
  - CTA: Sign up (60-90s)
- [ ] Tagline (60 characters max)
- [ ] Description (brief)
- [ ] Full description (detailed)

**Tagline Options:**
1. "Your clipboard, everywhere you need it."
2. "Sync smarter, work faster."
3. "Never lose a copy again."
4. "Clipboard management, evolved."

### Week 2: Build Hype & Community

**Build Pre-Launch Audience:**
- Add "Coming Soon" section to website with email signup
- Create Product Hunt launch page and share on social media
- Engage with Product Hunt community (comment on other launches)
- Build waitlist with incentives (early access, discount)

**Gather Testimonials:**
- Reach out to beta testers for quotes
- Get testimonials from diverse users (individual devs, teams, enterprises)
- Collect quotes that highlight different value propositions

**Prepare Launch Assets:**
- Draft launch post
- Prepare comment responses for common questions
- Create launch graphics for social media
- Write social media posts for launch day

## Launch Day Strategy

### Timing

**Best Launch Times:**
- **Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday**: 12:01 AM PST
- Avoid: Mondays (busy), Fridays (low engagement), holidays
- Launch at 12:01 AM PST to maximize time on homepage

**Launch Day Schedule:**

**12:01 AM PST (Launch)**
- Submit product to Product Hunt
- Post on social media (Twitter, LinkedIn, etc.)
- Email subscriber list
- Share in relevant Slack communities
- Comment immediately on your own post

**6:00 AM PST**
- Second wave of social media posts
- Share in developer forums (Reddit, Discord, etc.)
- Ping any influencers/advocates

**9:00 AM PST**
- Morning email blast to subscribers
- Share in company Slack/Discord
- Engage with Product Hunt community

**12:00 PM PST**
- Lunchtime social media push
- Check engagement metrics
- Respond to all comments

**3:00 PM PST**
- Afternoon social media posts
- Engage with late-day visitors
- Thank commenters and supporters

**6:00 PM PST**
- Evening social media posts
- Final push before end of day
- Prepare for next day

**9:00 PM PST**
- Review day's performance
- Plan next day's strategy
- Respond to any remaining comments

**Common Questions to Prepare For:**

**Q: How is this different from [competitor]?**
A: Great question! Unlike [competitor], ClipSync:
- Was built specifically for developers with 20+ text transformations
- Offers real-time team collaboration (unique to us)
- Has native desktop apps (not just browser-based)
- Includes enterprise-grade security features

**Q: Is my clipboard content secure?**
A: Absolutely! All synced content is end-to-end encrypted. We also offer per-clip password protection and local-first storage. Your data stays yours.

**Q: Can I use it offline?**
A: Yes! ClipSync works offline by default with local storage. Sync happens automatically when you're back online.

**Q: Do you support my device/IDE?**
A: We support desktop (Windows, Mac, Linux), mobile (iOS, Android), browsers (Chrome, Firefox), and IDEs (VS Code, Vim, Neovim). More integrations coming soon!

**Q: What's the free tier like?**
A: The free tier includes 1,000 clip history, basic sync, and core transformations. It's perfect for personal use and getting started.

**Q: Can I export my data?**
A: Yes! You can export all your clips at any time. We believe in data portability.

### Upvoting Strategy

**Who to Ask for Upvotes:**
- Your email subscriber list
- Your social media followers
- Your Slack/Discord communities
- Your beta testers
- Your friends and family
- Fellow developers in your network

**How to Ask:**
- Send personal, authentic messages
- Explain why you built it (your story)
- Don't spam or demand upvotes
- Follow up with gratitude

**Example Message:**
"Hey [Name], I'm launching ClipSync on Product Hunt today! It's the clipboard manager I built because I was tired of emailing code to myself. Would love your support if you have a moment: [Product Hunt link]"

**What NOT to Do:**
- Don't offer incentives for upvotes
- Don't create fake accounts
- Don't spam unrelated communities
- Don't beg or guilt-trip
- Don't ask multiple times
